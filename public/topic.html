AI-Driven Propaganda and Social Media Manipulation

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how information (and even more importantly, disinformation) spreads online. What once required many people to run propaganda campaigns can now be automated, scaled, and made way more convincing with AI.

From Manual Bots to AI-Driven Influence

Social bots have influenced public opinion for over a decade already. Researchers found that bots generated a significant share of election-related content during the 2016 US presidential election and the Brexit referendum, often orchestating coordinated, state linked narratives.

What has really changed is the scale and its sophistication. A 2025 case documented by the CSIS revealed that some Russian operators used an AI tool called "Meliorator" to create over a thousand fake American social media profiles, generating believable posts and replies matching each persona's apparent interests, which is something manual bot farms never did convincingly.

How It Works Today

There are 3 main points in this manner:

Bot farms at industrial scale: Modern operations use thousands of real smartphones running scripts that behave like genuine users, exploiting algorithms that reward engagement over accuracy. By 2024, automated bot traffic made up the majority of all web traffic for the first time in the history of the Internet.

AI-generated content at scale: Where older campaigns relied on repetitive, flagged posts, large language models (LLMs) now generate varied, contextual, and emotionally tuned content that is slowly becoming indistinguishable from real comments, even across different languages and regions.

Deepfakes as psychological weapons: During recent conflicts, including the war in Ukraine and tensions in the Middle East, deepfake videos and synthetic battlefield media have been used to fabricate military victories, falsify official statements, and create confusion among both soldiers and civilian populations, a tactic researchers have called a "cognitive weapon."

Why did I choose this topic?

To me, this topic is one of the most (if not THE MOST) important priority as a society to address when it comes down to AI. The ability to manipulate public opinion and spread disinformation at scale can have SERIOUS consequences not only for democracy, but also for social behaviour, our way of thinking, and even international relations. Understanding the mechanisms behind AI-driven propaganda is essential for developing effective countermeasures and promoting media literacy among the public.
